What is Peer Data Clipping?

Peer Data Clipping (PDC) refers to our proposed process of removing sensitive data related to other individuals from shared data streams. The clipping process would allow for privacy protection during peer-to-peer data sharing (of text, audio, video and 3D) for near real-time rendering of Smart City models using 3D data collected from multiple perspectives. Data flagged as private would be encrypted using Privacy-Preserving Face Recognition.[1] The shared city data model would not provide access to identifiable individuals without the use of multiple decryption keys held by a committee of peers. Individuals would have the option to decrypt data related to themselves.

Narrative Clip 2012 to 2016 - Consumer focused "Life-Logging" Camera

The Narrative Clip was initiated in 2012 by a $500,000 kickstarter campaign. A small "life-logging" camera automatically took a picture every 30 seconds. Narrative shipped 40,000 units of the Narrative 1 and 2 combined.[2] When the company could not attract further financing in 2016, only pre-ordered Narrative 2 units were delivered. The Narrative Clip 2 was packed with new features: video capture, Bluetooth control, a built-in Wifi cloud uploader and modular attachment system.]

Why, at the peak of it's potential, was the Narative Clip unable to move forward? "Due to cashflow problems and a broad decline in venture capital interest in wearables during 2016, the original company had to shut down late 2016. The assets and cloud service were acquired by a new company called Third Dot, run by some of the former founders and employees, which is keeping the service running for the existing users as well as maintaining sales of the Clip 2." [3]

Updates planned by Narrative:

Privacy Protection Indicator Colors

How can we make the data capture process more transparent, so near-by individuals are comfortable and aware of the degree to which their actions are recorded? Perhaps the existing "red" video recording indicator color could be expanded to include other types of recording.

At the most basic level, identity protected recording (green) would be air quality, humidity and temperature, wind, estimates based on sound, general counts of people, sound level, etc. At the highest level, another color simultaneous to green, would indicate software removal of identity features - blurring or removing people, omitting text and audio snippets.


Green - Identity protected recording (1 click)

Yellow - Speech-to-text recording (2 clicks)

Orange - Audio recording (3 clicks)

Red - Video recording (1 click, long)

Purple - Spatial data collection. 3D surfaces including faces (2 clicks, last one long)

Blue - AI managed privacy using device-as-service APIs. (3 clicks, last one long)

Color Combinations

Green and Yellow - Speech-to-text recording with generic identifiers removed.

Green, Orange and Red - Video and audio recording with generic identity filters.

Spatial Data with A.I. Filters
